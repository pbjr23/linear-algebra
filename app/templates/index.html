Quickstart

Learn the basics of how to use this app.

Entering matrices

Enter matrices in this format: [[1,2,3],[4,5,6],[7,8,9]].

This corresponds to the matrix $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 2 & 3\\4 & 5 & 6\\7 & 8 & 9\end{bmatrix}$


Generating random matrices

You can also generate random matrices in the following way: random(#rows, #columns, starting value (optional), ending value (optional)). If you do not specify a start and end range for the random values, they are assumed to be -10 and 10, respectively.

Examples:

random(2,2) could generate $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 2 & 3\\4 & 5 & 6\\7 & 8 & 9\end{bmatrix}$

random(3,2,100,150) could generate $\begin{bmatrix}107 & 116\\110 & 145\\132 & 142\end{bmatrix}$


Matrix operations

Matrices support many operations, such as +, -, *, ^, determinant(), transpose(), and eig().

Examples:

[[1,2,3],[4,5,6]] + [[3,3,3],[4,4,4]] would evalute to $\begin{bmatrix}4 & 5 & 6\\8 & 9 & 10\end{bmatrix}$

[[1,2],[4,5]] - [[1,1],[1,1]] would evalute to $\begin{bmatrix}0 & 1\\3 & 4\end{bmatrix}$

[[4,2],[6,5]] * [[2,6],[3,1]] would evalute to $\begin{bmatrix}14 & 26\\27 & 41\end{bmatrix}$

[[1,0],[6,5]] ^ 3 would evalute to $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\\186 & 125\end{bmatrix}$

transpose([[1,0],[6,5]]) would evalute to $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 6\\0 & 5\end{bmatrix}$

determinant([[1,2],[3,4]]) would evalute to -2

eig([[2, -4], [-1, -1]]) would evalute to 3.0, -2.0

About

What is this?

Linear algebra toolkit is a Flask app created by Prakhar Bhandari and Adel Qalieh. Along with this web interface, it features a CLI for doing matrix operations directly on the command line.
